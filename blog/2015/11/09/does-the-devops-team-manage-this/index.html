Does the DevOps Team Manage This?

Nov 9th, 2015 7:37 pm

This is a question that was asked at a recent Meetup I attended, but it was not the first time I had heard a question like this. I’ve actually had some recent discussions with peers around this very topic: “is there such a thing as a DevOps team or special DevOps positions?”

DevOps isn’t necessarily about creating a new team to write your Chef cookbooks, Puppet modules, Ansible playbooks, Jenkins workflows, Docker images, etc. While it may be necessary to pull together some willing volunteers to help jumpstart your efforts, this should be a temporory solution.

Longterm success with DevOps methodologies is about blurring the line between devs and ops (as well as many other areas of the business too - let’s not forget about security, networking, etc.). What do I mean by “blurring the line between devs and ops”? Ops teams need to start using common development practices. They need to use a version control system (i.e. Git, SVN, etc.); they need to test their configurations and scripts using a continuous integration pipeline. Also, dev teams should be more familar with common operations practices. A development team should know how their application is deployed; they should be familiar with how OS/platform configurations effect their applications performance.

Just like applications that you or your company may create and develop though, many of these tools that you’re using should have product owners. You need someone to take ownership of these products to ensure they’re available, up-to-date, and providing the needs of users. These products and tools will become more and more important to keep you’re business up and running and successful so you want to make sure they’re always available.
